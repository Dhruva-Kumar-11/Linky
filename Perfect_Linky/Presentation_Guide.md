# Linky Presentation Guide (For Teachers & Classmates)

## 🎤 The "Elevator Pitch"
"Linky is a serverless file-sharing tool that uses the same technology as Zoom (WebRTC) to let people send files directly browser-to-browser. It's faster, more private, and easier to use than traditional cloud storage because it removes the middleman entirely."

## 🏫 Key Learning Points (For Teachers)
*   **Networking:** Demonstrates **Peer-to-Peer (P2P)** architecture vs. Client-Server architecture.
*   **Security:** Showcases **End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)** and how we can use math (Hashing) to hide secret PINs from the internet.
*   **Web Development:** A masterclass in "Vanilla" JavaScript—building a complex app without using heavy frameworks, keeping it fast and lightweight.
*   **UI/UX:** Focuses on **Glassmorphism**, a modern design trend that uses blur and transparency to make apps look "premium" and clean.

## 🤝 Explaining it to Friends
*   **"It's like AirDrop, but for the whole internet."**
*   **"No accounts, no spam, no limits."**
*   **"Your files stay on your device until they reach your friend's device."**

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions (Simplified)
*   **Does it save my files?** No. Files live in your browser's memory only during the transfer.
*   **Is it safe?** Yes! It's encrypted twice. It's like putting your file in a locked box, inside a locked truck.
*   **What if I lose the PIN?** Just refresh and make a new one. Linky is ephemeral—it lives for the moment.
